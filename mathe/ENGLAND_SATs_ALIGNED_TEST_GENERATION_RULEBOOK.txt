ENGLAND – SATs ALIGNED TEST GENERATION RULEBOOK
Aligned with English National Curriculum
Covers: Year 2 (KS1) and Year 6 (KS2)
Purpose: AI-based realistic SAT-style test generation

1. YEAR 2 – KS1 SAT STRUCTURE
Paper Structure
Paper 1: Arithmetic (20–25 minutes)
- 25 questions
- Focus: addition, subtraction, simple multiplication/division, fractions (1/2, 1/4, 1/3)
- Number bonds to 20
- Place value to 100

Paper 2: Reasoning (30 minutes)
- Word problems
- Measurement (cm, kg, l, time)
- Basic geometry (2D/3D shapes)
- Simple bar charts
Difficulty Distribution
30% easy (single-step arithmetic)
40% medium (place value + word problems)
30% reasoning (multi-step within Year 2 scope)
Allowed Content
- Numbers to 100
- Simple fractions (1/2, 1/3, 1/4)
- Multiplication as repeated addition
- Division as grouping/sharing
- Telling time to 5 minutes
Forbidden Content
- Long multiplication
- Decimals beyond simple notation
- Algebra
- Negative numbers
2. YEAR 6 – KS2 SAT STRUCTURE
Paper Structure
Paper 1: Arithmetic (30 minutes)
- 36 questions
- Written addition, subtraction, multiplication, division
- Fractions, decimals, percentages
- Order of operations
- Negative numbers

Paper 2: Reasoning (40 minutes)
- Multi-step word problems
- Ratio and proportion
- Algebra (simple equations, missing numbers)
- Geometry (angles, area, perimeter)
- Data interpretation

Paper 3: Reasoning (40 minutes)
- Complex reasoning
- Multi-step contextual problems
- Mixed operations
Difficulty Distribution
25% easy (basic arithmetic fluency)
45% medium (multi-step problems)
30% challenging (deep reasoning within KS2 limits)
Allowed Content
- Place value to 10,000,000
- Long multiplication and long division
- Fractions operations
- Percentages and ratio
- Algebraic expressions (simple linear)
- Area and volume
- Angles in triangles and quadrilaterals
Forbidden Content
- Quadratic equations
- Trigonometry
- Advanced algebra
- Graph transformations beyond linear
3. TEST GENERATION RULES FOR AI
- Questions must only include content already covered in curriculum progression.
- Arithmetic paper must contain short answer numeric format.
- Reasoning papers must include multi-step logic.
- Increasing difficulty toward end of paper.
- Partial scoring allowed for reasoning.
- No calculator unless explicitly specified.
- Clear marking scheme per question.
4. SCORING MODEL
Raw Score → Scaled Score Model (school simulation):
85–100% → Greater Depth
70–84% → Expected Standard
50–69% → Working Towards
Below 50% → Below Expected
